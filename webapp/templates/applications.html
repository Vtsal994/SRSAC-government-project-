Department-wise Applications of Space Technology

Overview of Space Technology Applications in Rajasthan

Space technology, encompassing Remote Sensing (RS) and Geographic Information Systems (GIS), plays a crucial role in providing essential information for the sustainable management of resources and supporting various developmental activities across different departments in Rajasthan. [4, 10] It offers synoptic views of large areas, repetitive coverage, and multi-spectral data with variable resolution, making it an indispensable tool for planners and administrators. [10, 12]

The applications span multiple sectors, including agriculture, forestry, environment, land and water resources, rural and urban development, mining, transport, infrastructure, and more. [13] Common uses involve mapping for monitoring, tracing, measuring, assessment, identification, and modeling, contributing significantly to informed decision-making and efficient resource management. [13]

2.1 Agriculture/Horticulture Department

Remote sensing technology is extensively used in agriculture for monitoring seasonal patterns of crops, assessing productivity, and managing variable climatic conditions. [16] It provides timely information crucial for agricultural planning, drought declaration, and crop forecasting. [16] Applications include land use, crop type identification, yield prediction, soil type mapping, and estimation of crop water requirements, among others. [16]

SRSAC has been vital in assisting agricultural policy decisions by providing timely statistics on crop production and forecasting. [17] Key projects include the "Crop Acreage and Production Estimation (CAPE)" in collaboration with Space Applications Centre (SAC) and ISRO, which estimated wheat and mustard acreage across various districts from 1989 onwards. [17] The Centre also contributed to the Integrated Mission for Sustainable Development (IMSD) and projects for estimating crop residue burned areas and generating soil fertility maps across Rajasthan. [17] Currently, SRSAC supports the FASAL project by providing ground-based data for crop acreage estimation. [17]

2.2 Forest Department

Space-based inputs have significantly contributed to the sustainable management of environmental resources, including forest cover mapping and monitoring, biodiversity characterization, and forest fire detection. [19]

Past Initiatives:
  • SRSAC created multiple versions of forest-related data based on the requirements of the State Forest & Environment Department. [19]
  • Forest areas mapping and density-wise classification for Bundi district were carried out during 1996-97. [19]
  • A District-level/State-level resource Atlas of Rajasthan was prepared in 2007. [19]
  • Approximately 1500 forest block maps were prepared in digital format using satellite data, and later, forest block mapping and classification were done at a 1:10000 scale using Cartosat-1 2.5-meter spatial resolution data. [19]
Current Work:

SRSAC Rajasthan is actively engaged in a project to analyze spatial changes in forest cover areas within the Jodhpur Division over the years. [19]

Future Vision:

The Centre aims to extend the methodology for estimating forest cover changes to the entire state of Rajasthan, building on the success of the Jodhpur Division project. [19]

2.3 Environment Department

Geospatial technology is widely used globally for environmental data analysis and planning, enabling the derivation of various environmental parameters from remote sensing data. [21] It aids in preventing environmental hazards, monitoring, and mitigation, and is also used for generating environmental models, air pollution control, disaster management, and wastewater management. [21]

Past Initiatives:
  • SRSAC prepared inputs for environmental studies for Sanganer, submitted to the National Productivity Council (NPC), New Delhi. [21]
  • Basemap and LULC maps were prepared for the proposed Power Generating Plant at Mahi, Banswara, submitted to Nuclear Power Corporation of India Limited (NPCIL). [21]
  • Land Use Land Cover Analysis was conducted for areas around Kasnau-Matasukh Mines (Nagaur), Kayad Mines (Ajmer), and Barsingsar Mines (Bikaner). [21]
  • A green cover assessment project was completed for Chanderiya Lead Zinc Smelter plant (Chittorgarh) using very high-resolution satellite data. [21]
Current Work:

Wetlands mapping is an ongoing critical environmental project, with a state-wide wetland inventory being created in collaboration with ISRO, producing remote sensing-based mapping products for 100 selected wetlands in Rajasthan. [21] Following the successful green-cover assessment for Chanderiya Lead Zinc Smelter, Hindustan Zinc Ltd. has commissioned SRSAC to assess green-cover for seven more sites using very high-resolution satellite images. [21]

Future Vision:

SRSAC intends to apply geospatial technology for various environmental mapping tasks, especially with rising concerns about climate change and its impact on crop production. [22] The Centre has also proposed providing GIS inputs for building climate-resilient structures under the MGNREGS national-level project. [22]

1.1 Watershed Development & Soil Conservation Department

Remote sensing is instrumental in generating environmental indicators for delineating ridgelines, characterizing erosion-prone areas, and creating locale-specific action plans for land and water resources. [23]

Past Initiatives:
  • Since 1985, efforts were made to map water harvesting structures (anicuts) and ravines across Rajasthan. [23]
  • Land-use-land-cover mapping was carried out at a scale of 1:2,50,000 in collaboration with the Department of Space, Government of India. [23]
  • Integrated resource surveys were conducted to combat drought, and a World Bank-assisted project in 1992 developed a GIS-based watershed management information system. [23]
  • In 2004, panchayat samiti-wise macro and micro watersheds were delineated for the entire state. [23]
  • Mapping of thematic layers under the Integrated Watershed Management Programme (IWMP) and preparation of a watershed atlas were completed in 2010 and 2011, respectively. [23]
  • An impact assessment of a watershed project for Seva Mandir, Udaipur, was conducted in 2016. [23]
Current Work:

Currently, the Centre is monitoring Integrated Watershed Management Programme (IWMP) watersheds using geospatial technologies in collaboration with NRSC/ISRO/DoS, Govt. of India. [23]

1.11 Education Department

The Centre has mapped approximately 1.20 lakh school locations across Rajasthan with associated attribute data. This data helps identify gap areas to strengthen school education. [32]

1.12 Energy Department

Energy is a key growth indicator for any state. Geospatial support helps in optimally positioning solar and wind energy stations, promoting non-conventional energy sources like sun, wind, and biomass. [33]

1.13 Election Commission
