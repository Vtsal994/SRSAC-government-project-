About SRSAC

Introduction

The State Remote Sensing Application Centre (SRSAC), Jodhpur, was established in 1985 by the Department of Science & Technology, Government of Rajasthan. Initially, it operated as the Aerial Photo Interpretation Laboratory (APIL) under the Desert Development Programme (1979), with the aim to assist soil and water conservation planning in the State, under the State Agriculture Department. In 1985, recognizing the multi-disciplinary utilities of Remote Sensing technology, the laboratory was transferred to the State Department of Science & Technology and officially renamed as State Remote Sensing Application Centre (SRSAC).

SRSAC functions as the geospatial hub for Rajasthan, providing spatial planning, monitoring, and decision-making solutions through geoinformatics to various state departments. The Centre has been instrumental in utilizing remote sensing and GIS technologies for sustainable development in Rajasthan, collaborating extensively with national agencies like the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the Department of Space, Government of India. [4] It has contributed to projects such as land-use-land-cover mapping, crop-forecasting, acreage estimation, wetland mapping, wasteland mapping, groundwater prospects, and groundwater quality mapping.

Mandate

The Centre's mandate encompasses catering to the natural resource survey, monitoring, and planning needs of Rajasthan for over three decades. It actively participates in collaborative projects with the Department of Space and provides regular assistance to state user departments for planning development activities using Remote Sensing and GIS techniques.

  • To generate and manage spatial and temporal geospatial databases.
  • To provide RS-GIS services and products to government departments.
  • To support planning and implementation of development programs.
  • To collaborate with national agencies like NRSC, SAC, ISRO, and Regional Remote Sensing Centres (RRSC’s) for joint projects.
  • To conduct capacity building, trainings, and workshops.

Objectives

SRSAC aims to move from providing information support to offering decision support for sustainable development and management of natural resources, along with infrastructure development and urban planning, through the application of remote sensing and GIS technology.

  • Support decentralized planning through spatial data infrastructure.
  • Assist in agriculture, water resource, urban planning, forest, and mining studies.
  • Promote environment monitoring, green cover assessment, and wetland inventories.
  • Provide decision-support tools to policy makers through spatial analy
  • Generate databanks and information systems for Natural Resources.
  • Undertake short-term and long-term experimental and operational Remote Sensing studies for various Natural Resources, Infrastructure, and Urban Planning.
  • Popularize Remote Sensing and GIS by organizing training courses/workshops/seminars for different state user departments, technocrats, students, etc.

Infrastructure

The Centre is fully equipped with the latest infrastructure facilities necessary for conducting Remote Sensing and GIS-based studies. [5] This includes high-end GIS and remote sensing workstations, large-format scanners, plotters, UAVs, and GPS tools. It maintains spatial data servers, repositories, and archives of satellite imagery. The software environment supports a range of applications including ERDAS, ArcGIS, QGIS, eCognition, AutoCAD, and Python.

Achievements

Over the last three decades, SRSAC has worked on several user-oriented application projects and has achieved proficiency in geospatial technology, collaborating extensively with the Department of Space.

  • Successfully delivered over 120 space-based projects.
  • Generated numerous geospatial layers for information support.
  • Performed comprehensive mapping including:
    • Land Use & Land Cover (LULC) mapping.
    • Waterbodies mapping.
    • Wasteland mapping.
    • Land-degradation mapping.
    • Wetland mapping (mapped 100+ wetlands, monitored green cover and urban growth).
    • Forest blocks mapping.
    • Administrative boundaries demarcation.
  • Prepared various atlases: Resource Atlas of Rajasthan, Watershed Atlas, Ground Water Atlas, Ayurvedic Medicinal Plant Atlas, and Soil Resource Atlas of Rajasthan.
  • Georeferenced cadastral maps for 422 of 426 tehsils in Rajasthan.
  • Developed major thematic layers: LULC, soil, waterbodies, forest blocks, etc.
  • Successfully completed projects such as National Land Use Land Cover – 3rd Cycle (2015-16), Preparation of Ground Water Quality Layer under RGNDWMP Phase-IV, National Wasteland Change Analysis (2015-16), Empowering Panchayati Raj Institutions Spatially (EPRIS), Space-based Information Support for Decentralized Planning (SISDP), and National Geomorphological & Lineament Mapping (NGLM).
  • Monitored the landscape through change detection activities, including watershed monitoring, waterbodies monitoring, eco-sensitive zone monitoring, and LULC change detection around mining areas.
  • Popularized remote sensing by organizing training courses, workshops, and seminars.
